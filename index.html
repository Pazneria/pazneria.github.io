AGI Timeline Analysis

Predicting when artificial general intelligence (AGI) will arrive is exceptionally uncertain. Numerous surveys, expert forecasts, forecasting models and prediction markets offer a wide range of dates. Surveys of AI researchers and scientists before large language models suggested median timelines between the 2040s and 2060s【40782381957888†L154-L163】【155391981783607†L149-L156】; the 2012/2013 and 2009 surveys predicted a 50 % chance between 2040 – 2050 and AGI around 2050【40782381957888†L273-L287】. More recent surveys like the 2023 Expert Survey on Progress in AI estimate a 50 % chance of high‑level machine intelligence by 2047, thirteen years earlier than the 2022 survey’s 2060 estimate【509586981814789†L200-L208】. Meanwhile, prediction markets such as Metaculus give a median of 2031 for the first generally capable AI and 2027 for weakly general AI【456069622215296†L49-L52】. Expert opinions vary widely: some, like Andrew Critch, predict AGI within a few years (2026), while others, like Ege Erdil, place it as far out as the 2070s【456069622215296†L61-L88】. Overall, our dataset of 31 predictions has a mean arrival year of about 2051 and a median of 2045. Given this diversity of perspectives, we select 1 January 2045 as a central estimate for the AGI countdown. This choice balances survey medians trending earlier (around 2047) and the spread of expert and market forecasts.
